The Story Behind Velocity

There was always a pull toward this industry. Even in high school, something about the plants, the pipelines, the sheer scale of what oil and gas builds had a strong pull. Getting in was the question — not whether.

College started in welding. Did not finish. But learning never stopped. What the classroom offered was kept — and then went further on my own. A practice setup was built in the backyard. Rig welders willing to teach were sought out — men who had run thousands of joints in conditions not yet seen — and every lesson was absorbeded everything they would share. When the time felt right to test, there was no waiting for someone to hand over an opportunity. Laboratory time was paid for out of pocket pocket — went in on my own time, ran test coupons, pushed them through bend tests until passing was certain. Not because anyone required it. Because the standard required it.

That discipline opened the door. Starting as a helper in fabrication shops in South Houston — places where they built machinery parts, structural components, and industrial assemblies in the kind of environment where you either learn fast or get left behind. Head down, eyes open — every detail was watcheded how the experienced hands approached fit-up, how they moved around a joint, how they read a drawing. Then the welding came — and the preparation was there when it did.

From those shops the work kept moving — one plant, then another. Each step brought something the last one could not. The workrk took me across South Houston, through the Ship Channel, into South Texas, out to the wide-open spreads of West Texas, and eventually all the way up to the oilfields of North Dakota where the winters hit hard and the pace never slows. High-pressure alloy lines. Critical systems. Environments where a substandard weld is not just a quality failure — it is a safety event. Every joint signed off on carries full accountability behind it.

That is where the foundation was built. Not in a classroom. In fabrication shops, in the field, across every climate this industry runs in. By the time the path moved into QC and compliance, there was already a deep understanding of what it means to be accountable for a weld from thenside out — because that work had been done firsthand.

As the industry grew, so did the demands. Tighter specifications. More exotic alloys. Stricter jurisdictional requirements. Clients who needed someone who could stand in front of a National Board team and defend every decision in the quality system. The move was from the torch to the clipboard. From the joint to the QC Manual. From the floor to the ASME Joint Review tablee — and everything from those years came withh me.

But something else was shifting at the same time. Technology was rewriting the rules of the trade. Digital documentation. AI analysis. Real-time data books. The next generation walking into this industry today is entering a world where a smart platform can flag a reject before the film comes back — where the entire QC package from first weld to system handover lives in purpose-built software, not a binder on a foreman's truck.

The belief is that the next generation deserves a new foundation. One that honours the discipline, the heat, the accountability earned one test coupon at a time — but arms clients with tools that match the pace of a modern industrial world. That is exactly why Velocity's platform was built from the ground up.

Velocity is not an inspection service. It is a consulting practice built on lived experience — from South Houston fab shops to the Northern Plains, from helper to National Board stamp — and a belief that the future of this industry belongs to those willing to lay a new foundation for the ones coming up behind them.

Common Security Questions
Can Velocity QA/QC access client inspection data? +
No — it's technically impossible. Your data is encrypted on your device using a key derived from your passphrase. That passphrase never leaves your machine. Even if Velocity QA/QC wanted to access your data, the encryption key does not exist anywhere outside your browser session.
What happens if the passphrase is lost? +
The passphrase cannot be recovered by anyone — that's the security guarantee. We recommend keeping it in a secure password manager (1Password, Bitwarden) and making regular encrypted backups using the platform's built-in export. Treat your passphrase the same way you'd treat the combination to a physical safe.
Does this meet API 510/570 record-keeping requirements? +
Yes. API 510 §7.8.2 and API 570 §7.8 require that inspection records be maintained in a retrievable format. The Velocity platform satisfies this by producing formal inspection reports that can be printed as PDF, exported as tamper-evident encrypted files, or printed to hard copy — all accepted record formats under the API inspection codes.
Our company has IT security requirements. Will this pass review? +
For clients with formal vendor security reviews, the on-premise deployment option eliminates all external exposure. The platform can be hosted on your internal network with no internet connectivity required. We can provide technical documentation of the encryption implementation for your IT security team's review.
How can the encryption be verified as real and working? +
Yes. After entering any data and saving, open your browser's developer tools → Application → Local Storage. You will see the stored value as a base64-encoded ciphertext string — completely unreadable without the passphrase. This confirms encryption is active. The platform source code is available for independent technical review on request.
